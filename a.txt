The cargo ship drifted slowly into the harbor just before dawn.
Rust had formed along the lower hull, eating through decades of paint.
A single light flickered on the bridge, casting pale shadows on the water.
The dock workers arrived at six, hauling crates down the narrow gangway.
By noon, half the cargo had been unloaded and sorted into the warehouse.
The captain signed the manifest without reading it, as he always did.
Rain began to fall around three, turning the concrete slick and gray.
Nobody noticed the small crate left behind near the stern.
